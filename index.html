The Reality of Obesity
Obesity doesn't wait.
Neither should you.

Heart Disease
Starts earlier than you think
Arterial plaque buildup from obesity can begin in your 20s. High blood pressure and elevated cholesterol are now commonly diagnosed in patients under 30.
Type 2 Diabetes
Prediabetes affects 1 in 3 adults
Over 96 million Americans have prediabetes — and 80% don't know it. Obesity is the single strongest predictor. Losing just 5–7% of body weight cuts your risk of progression by 58%.
Joint & Bone Health
Every pound adds 4× pressure on knees
Osteoarthritis is accelerated by excess weight at any age. Young adults with obesity face a significantly higher lifetime risk of joint replacement surgery before age 50.
Mental Health
55% higher risk of depression
The link between obesity and depression is bidirectional — each worsens the other. Inflammation from excess body fat directly impacts brain chemistry and mood regulation.
Sleep Apnea
Obesity is the #1 cause
Up to 45% of adults with obesity have sleep apnea. Untreated, it raises the risk of heart attack, stroke, and cognitive decline — and is now being diagnosed in patients in their early 20s.
Liver Disease
NAFLD affects 1 in 4 people globally
Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease is now the most common liver condition worldwide, driven almost entirely by obesity and insulin resistance. It can progress silently to cirrhosis without symptoms.

Sermorelin Injections
Coming Soon
Growth Hormone Stimulator

Sermorelin is a growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) analog. Rather than injecting synthetic growth hormone directly, sermorelin signals your own pituitary gland to produce more natural growth hormone — a safer, more physiologic approach.

How It Works at Vital Metabolic

We test your IGF-1 (growth hormone marker) at your initial visit. If levels are suboptimal, we prescribe sermorelin as a subcutaneous injection — typically given nightly before bed. Levels are rechecked every 3 months and dose is adjusted based on your results and body composition changes.

Testosterone Therapy
Coming Soon
For Men & Women

Low testosterone isn't just a men's issue. Women in their 20s and 30s can have suboptimal testosterone levels that cause fatigue, weight gain, low libido, depression, and muscle loss. We test total and free testosterone and design therapy that fits your biology.

For Women Too — Not Just Men

Women naturally have lower testosterone than men, but still depend on it for energy, muscle tone, libido, and mood. Low-dose testosterone therapy in women is often dramatically effective — and is frequently missed or dismissed by conventional doctors. We test free and total testosterone as part of your initial labs.

How We Monitor Testosterone Therapy

We check total testosterone, free testosterone, estradiol, hematocrit, and PSA (men) at baseline and every 3 months. Dose is carefully titrated to keep you in the optimal range — not just "normal," but optimal for your age and goals.

NAD+ is the master molecule of cellular energy. It powers every cell's mitochondria, drives DNA repair, and is essential for metabolism. Levels drop dramatically with age, stress, and poor diet — often by 50% by age 40.

Please read the following before booking an appointment or beginning any treatment at Vital Metabolic.

General Medical Disclaimer

The content on this website is for informational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. All treatment decisions are made on an individual basis following a comprehensive in-person evaluation, physical examination, and laboratory testing by our physician. Results vary between individuals. Vital Metabolic does not guarantee specific outcomes.

Off-Label Prescribing Notice

Some therapies offered at Vital Metabolic — including Sermorelin, Testosterone (in women), and certain peptides — may be prescribed for uses not specifically approved by the FDA ("off-label"). Off-label prescribing is a legal and common medical practice. All off-label treatments at this clinic are supported by clinical evidence, prescribed by a board-certified physician, and fully disclosed to patients prior to initiation.

Compounded Medications Notice

Certain medications prescribed at Vital Metabolic, including Sermorelin and some peptides, are prepared by FDA-registered compounding pharmacies. Compounded medications are not FDA-approved finished drug products but are legally prepared under Section 503A/503B of the Federal Food, Drug & Cosmetic Act. Patients will be informed and will provide written consent before receiving any compounded medication.

Informed Consent

All patients at Vital Metabolic are required to provide written informed consent before beginning any treatment. This includes a discussion of the treatment's purpose, benefits, risks, alternatives, and monitoring plan. Consent is obtained in person at your initial visit. You have the right to ask questions, decline any treatment, or discontinue at any time.

Controlled Substances Notice

Testosterone is a Schedule III controlled substance. Its prescription and dispensing is governed by the DEA and state pharmacy regulations. Vital Metabolic complies fully with all federal and Texas state laws governing controlled substance prescribing, documentation, and monitoring. Testosterone therapy requires ongoing lab monitoring and cannot be prescribed without an in-person evaluation.

Cash-Pay & No Insurance

Vital Metabolic is a cash-pay only clinic. We do not bill insurance, Medicare, or Medicaid. By paying directly, patients receive transparent pricing with no surprise charges. Some treatments — including off-label therapies — may not be covered by insurance regardless of provider. We can provide receipts for patients who wish to submit for out-of-network reimbursement at their own discretion.

Texas Medical Board Compliance

Vital Metabolic operates under the full licensure and oversight of the Texas Medical Board. Our physician holds active board certifications from the American Board of Obesity Medicine (ABOM) and the American Board of Pediatrics (ABP). Complaints or concerns may be directed to the Texas Medical Board at www.tmb.state.tx.us or (512) 305-7010. This notice is provided in accordance with Texas Medical Board rules.
